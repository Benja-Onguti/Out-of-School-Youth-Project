Empowering Out of School Youth

The Hands of Care and Hope Initiative (HCH) is a community-based project dedicated to uplifting marginalized residents in the Huruma and Kariobangi regions of Nairobi. While the initiative provides a foundation for the future by putting 1,095 children through formal school, it also addresses the immediate crisis facing "out-of-school" youth. By utilizing the Mother Kevin Youth Centre, HCH offers a safe haven and rehabilitative programs for young adults (18–26) battling hopelessness and anti-social behaviors, helping them transition from the fringes of society back into active community life.

About US

Empowering Youth, Building Futures

Managed by the Franciscan Missionary Sisters for Africa, we serve marginalized communities in Huruma, Madoya, Korogocho, and Kariobangi North.

Our mission in Nairobi’s informal settlements is to turn 'hopelessness' into 'opportunity.' Our youth program reaches out to 18–26-year-olds who have fallen through the cracks of the education system. Through a mix of vocational training, behavior change mentorship, and youth-led evangelism, we give them the tools to build a career and a better life. We’re here to ensure that every young person in our community has the chance to become self-sufficient and proud of their path.

Our Mission

Empowering youths holistically in the slums of korogocho, Huruma and Madoya through life skills sessions, mentorship and sponsorship for technical Vocational training for employment opportunities

Our Vision

To eradicate poverty by empowering marginalized youths with skills and opportunities.

Our Goals

Help young people find their spark again. Through technical skills and personal mentorship, we’re helping them move from just surviving to building a future where they are independent, resilient, and successful.
